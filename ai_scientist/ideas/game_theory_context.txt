THEORETICAL BACKGROUND FOR GAME-THEORETIC MOE:

Core Mathematical Frameworks:
1. Von Neumann-Morgenstern Game Theory
   - Minimax theorem for zero-sum games
   - Nash equilibrium existence (Kakutani fixed point)
   - Connection to linear programming duality

2. Information Theory & Maximum Entropy
   - Shannon entropy as special case (q→1)
   - Tsallis entropy and q-exponential families
   - Rényi entropy connections

3. Optimal Transport Theory
   - Kantorovich duality
   - Wasserstein distances
   - Connection to distributionally robust optimization

4. Online Learning Theory
   - No-regret learning (Hannan consistency)
   - Mirror descent in Banach spaces
   - Multiplicative weights and entropic regularization

Key Theorems to Reference:
- Sion's minimax theorem (infinite dimensions)
- Blackwell's approachability theorem
- Cover's universal portfolio theorem
- Entropic CLT (Barron, 1986)

Important Connections:
- Quantal Response Equilibrium (McKelvey & Palfrey)
- Maximum entropy inverse RL (Ziebart et al.)
- Distributionally robust optimization (Ben-Tal et al.)
- Information-theoretic bounded rationality (Ortega & Braun)
